GOOGLE ADS

Stop Burning Money on Bad Keywords

Most contractors set up Google Ads wrong. They bid on broad terms, waste budget, and quit. Here's what actually works.

🎯

High-Intent Keywords Only

Target "paver installer near me", "roof repair [city]", "HVAC emergency [zip]". These people are ready to buy. We saw 25%+ conversion rates.

💰

Budget: $30–$50/Day to Start

Start with $30-$50/day on 5-10 exact-match keywords. Scale what works. Kill what doesn't. We hit $100K spending only $1,200/month on ads.

📍

Tight Geo-Targeting

Set a 15-25 mile radius around your service area. Exclude areas you won't drive to. Every dollar goes to people who can actually hire you.

📱

Call-Only Campaigns

For contractors, phone calls beat form fills. Set up call-only ads that ring your phone directly. Our cost per call averaged $8-$12.

servicegrowth.ai05
GOOGLE ADS PRO TIPS

The Details That 10x Your Results

  • Add negative keywords religiously — "DIY", "jobs", "salary", "how to" are costing you money
  • Run ads only during business hours (or when your AI chatbot captures after-hours leads)
  • Use ad extensions: location, call, sitelink, and callout — boosts CTR by 15-20%
  • Write ads that mention your city — "Jacksonville Paver Installation | Free Estimates"
  • Track conversions with call tracking numbers — if you're not measuring, you're guessing
  • Check search terms report weekly and add irrelevant terms as negatives

FACEBOOK ADS

The Before/After Strategy That Prints Leads

Facebook isn't like Google — people aren't searching for you. You need to stop the scroll with visuals that make homeowners say "I want that."

📸

Before/After Creative

Show dramatic transformations. Ugly driveway to beautiful paver patio. These get 3-5x more engagement than stock photos. Use real job site photos.

👥

Target Homeowners, Not Renters

Ages 30-65, homeowners, income $75K+, within 25 miles. Layer on interests like "home improvement", "HGTV", "Zillow". Exclude renters.

🔄

Retarget Everyone

Install the Facebook Pixel. Retarget everyone who visited your site but didn't call. These warm leads convert at 3-5x the rate of cold traffic.

📋

Lead Form Ads

Use Facebook's native lead forms — they auto-fill name, email, and phone. Less friction = more leads. We averaged $8-$15 per lead.

BRANDING PLAYBOOK

How to Position Yourself as High-End

  • Get a real logo — not a Canva template. It's the face of your company on every estimate, invoice, and ad
  • Pick 2-3 brand colors and use them everywhere. Consistency = trust. Inconsistency = amateur hour
  • Build a professional website that showcases your best work — not a free Wix site with stock photos
  • Wear branded uniforms on every job. Branded trucks, branded shirts, branded everything. It's a walking billboard
  • Write proposals, not text quotes. A professional PDF proposal with photos closes 2x more than "hey it'll be $8K"
  • Show your process — consultations, 3D renders, material selections. High-end clients want an experience, not just a service
  • Say no to projects that don't fit. Every $500 job you take is a $5K job you didn't have time for

FOLLOW-UP SYSTEM

The Fortune Is in the Follow-Up

80% of sales happen after the 5th follow-up. Most contractors follow up once — maybe twice — then give up.

📅

Day 0: Instant Confirmation

"Thanks for having us out! Here's a summary of what we discussed." Attach the quote immediately. Strike while the iron is hot.

📨

Day 2: Value Add

"Wanted to share photos of a similar project we recently completed in your area. Happy to answer any questions!" Show, don't tell.

🔔

Day 5: Gentle Nudge

"Just checking in on your project. Our calendar is filling up for next month. Any questions I can help with?" Light urgency.

⏰

Day 7: Urgency Close

"Last follow-up — we have [X] spots left this month. If you'd like to move forward, we can lock in your date today."

servicegrowth.ai19
PRO TIPS

Follow-Up Rules That Matter

  • Text beats email for contractors — 98% open rate for texts vs 20% for email
  • Always personalize — use their name, project type, and neighborhood
  • Include photos of similar completed projects in your follow-ups
  • Never be pushy — be helpful. "Any questions?" beats "Are you ready to sign?"
  • Automate everything. If you're manually following up, you'll forget. Set it and forget it.
  • Track which follow-up converts — for us, Day 5 had the highest close rate
